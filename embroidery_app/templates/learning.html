✦ Core Stitching Methods
🧵

Running Stitch (Taipchi)

The foundation of Chikankari. Simple in-and-out stitch done in straight lines to outline and fill motifs.

1
Thread needle with cotton thread
2
Insert needle from back to front
3
Push through at equal intervals
4
Continue in uniform rhythm
Chikankari · Kasuti
✦

Darning Stitch (Negi)

Primary stitch of Phulkari and Kasuti. Horizontal rows pass over and under warp threads creating geometric fills.

1
Count fabric threads carefully
2
Work from wrong side of fabric
3
Pass silk thread over warp threads
4
Return in opposite direction
Phulkari · Kasuti
🌸

Chain Stitch

Creates linked loops forming chains, used in Kutch embroidery for outlining motifs and decorative borders.

1
Bring thread to front at start
2
Hold thread to form a loop
3
Insert needle back at start point
4
Catch the loop with needle
Kutch Embroidery
💫

Shadow Work (Bakhiya)

Signature of Chikankari. Worked from the back of translucent fabric, creating soft shadow effects on the front.

1
Use transparent muslin or georgette
2
Work herringbone stitch on wrong side
3
Stitches show as shadow through fabric
4
Maintain even tension throughout
Chikankari
🔲

Applique (Gota Work)

Gold or silver ribbon cut into shapes and stitched onto fabric — foundation of Gota Patti from Rajasthan.

1
Cut gota ribbon to desired shape
2
Pin securely to base fabric
3
Slip stitch all edges to secure
4
Add zari thread accents
Gota Patti
🪟

Mirror Work (Shisha)

Attaching small mirrors to fabric using decorative stitching — hallmark of Kutch embroidery from Gujarat.

1
Place mirror on fabric
2
Secure with criss-cross base stitches
3
Work buttonhole stitch around edge
4
Ensure mirror is fully secured
Kutch Embroidery
